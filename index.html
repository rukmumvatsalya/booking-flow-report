Booking Flow Analysis

False Positive & False Negative Identification — with Progressive Confidence Model, UX Fixes & Trust Recovery

April 2026 7 FP + 6 FN cases 10 recommended fixes
Section 1

Objective

The objective of the Booking Flow is to identify if we are serviceable at the customer's location and, if yes, enable them to book a new internet connection. The flow must ensure that from the moment a customer launches the app to the point where a connection is installed, the serviceability determination is accurate and trustworthy.

Two success metrics define this:

Precision (Minimize FPs)
Every customer who pays should get a connection. A post-payment rejection is a trust-destroying event.
Recall (Minimize FNs)
Every serviceable customer should be able to book. A wrongful rejection is lost revenue and a lost relationship.
Section 2

Current Booking Flow

1
App Launch & Location Permission

Capture customer's current GPS coordinates. Assumption: customer wants connection at their current location.

2
Serviceability Check (Broad Area)

Area-level check — not pin-precise. If serviceable, proceed. If not, show "not serviceable" with option to change location manually.

3
Onboarding & Pricing

How the service works, booking fee, setup fee, recharge model for internet access.

4
Phone Number Collection

Customer provides their phone number to proceed.

5
Booking Fee Payment Money moves

Customer pays the booking fee.

6
Address Collection

Customer shares full address for CSP/partner navigation.

7
Location Confirmation Trust breaks here

"Are you at the location where you want the installation?" If yes, request shared with partner. If no, wait.

The critical gap: Payment (Step 5) sits between the broad check (Step 2) and the precise pin check (Step 7). Any disagreement between these two stages means the customer pays, then gets rejected.
Section 3

Key Structural Issues

1. Two-stage serviceability check with payment in between

The coarse area-level check happens before payment; the precise pin-level check happens after. Any disagreement results in a false positive — the customer pays, then gets rejected. This is the single biggest source of trust erosion.

2. GPS as the sole default location signal

GPS is convenient but imprecise, especially indoors. A single reading at a single moment is treated as ground truth — but it isn't. It drives both FPs (appears serviceable, isn't) and FNs (appears non-serviceable, is).

3. Assumption: current location = installation location

The flow assumes the customer wants the connection where they are right now. Many customers are at their office, travelling, or planning ahead. This drives both FPs and FNs.

4. Ambiguous "Are you at the location?" confirmation

Users treat this as a generic "next" button rather than a contractual confirmation. They don't understand it triggers a precise serviceability recheck with real consequences (cancellation and refund).

Section 6

Proposed Intervention: Pre-Payment Location Recheck

What Was Proposed

Capture the customer's location again in the background just before the payment process begins:

  • If doubtful: Confirm the location with the customer before asking for the booking fee.
  • If very different or wrong: Ask the customer to provide/select the location again.

Coverage Assessment

CaseCovered?How
FP1 Partial Second reading may correct a one-time GPS fluke. Consistently bad GPS (basement, metal roof) won't be caught.
FP2 Partial Catches if user has physically moved since launch. If still at the same non-installation location, GPS is the same.
FP3 Well Covered Background GPS reveals that user's actual position contradicts the area they manually selected.
FP4 Not Covered Both reads are still broad-area checks. The problem is granularity, not timing.
FP5 Not Covered Comprehension problem at confirmation step, not a location-detection problem.
FP6–7 Not Covered Temporal/physical issues unrelated to location accuracy.
FN1–6 Not Covered Users blocked at launch never reach payment. The recheck doesn't apply to them.
Verdict: The pre-payment recheck is worth implementing — it's low-cost and catches real drift/mismatch scenarios (FP1, FP2, FP3). But it's a tactical patch, not a structural fix. The biggest trust-killers (FP4, FP5) and the biggest funnel-killers (FN1, FN2, FN3) remain untouched.
Section 7

The Progressive Confidence Model

The current flow treats serviceability as a single yes/no gate at launch. In reality, location accuracy is probabilistic. No single GPS reading or area selection is reliable enough to make a commitment — especially one involving money.

Instead of one check, build a progressive confidence score from multiple signals collected through the flow. Each signal adds or reduces confidence. Decisions are triggered by confidence thresholds, not binary gates.

1
GPS at App Launch
Broad area serviceability — first approximation
2
User Self-Report
"I'm here" vs "different address" — intent signal, where do they actually want the connection?
3
Background GPS Before Payment
Drift detection — has the user moved since launch?
4
Address + Pin Entry (Before Payment)
Precise serviceability — the definitive check before money moves
5
GPS at Confirmation Step
Physical presence verification — passive check, compared to address

How Signals Interact

SignalsAction
All signals agree — same serviceable area High confidence — proceed smoothly
GPS says Area A, typed address in Area B Mismatch — "Aapka address aur location alag dikh raha hai — ek baar confirm kar lein"
Broad area serviceable, pin in partial-coverage zone Doubtful — "Aapke area mein service milne ki sambhavna hai — address se confirm hoga"
GPS accuracy > 500m Low confidence — Don't hard-block. Fallback to manual: "Hum aapki exact location nahi pakad paaye. Area search karein."

Section 9

Trust Recovery Layer

Even with all fixes, some false positives will slip through. For those cases, the refund experience should actively rebuild trust instead of breaking it further.

Principles

01
Auto-trigger refund

Don't make the user ask or call support. Refund should start the moment cancellation happens.

02
Show status proactively

The user should never wonder "where's my money?" — show refund status in-app immediately.

03
Turn failure into a lead

Capture intent for when coverage expands. The user becomes a warm lead, not a churned user.
